1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
This document provides comprehensive documentation for the HR Dataset Analysis
project. The project profiles 3,000+ employee records, tracking their employment
histories, performance scores, engagement levels, training costs, and work-life
balance. The findings offer human resources managers and executive teams 
data-backed guidance for improving corporate learning programs, enhancing employee
performance, and analyzing employee retention patterns.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. DATA CLEANSING & PREPROCESSING PIPELINE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw HR data contains errors, missing values, and duplication. We designed
and executed a robust data cleaning pipeline:

A. Date Parsing:
   Dates were originally loaded as string objects. We parsed the following
   fields to standard datetime format (YYYY-MM-DD):
   - StartDate (e.g., "20-Sep-19" -> "2019-09-20")
   - ExitDate (includes NaT for active employees)
   - DOB (Date of Birth)
   - Training Date
   - Survey Date

B. Null Value Pruning:
   - Analysis of null percentages revealed that the "Survey Date" column was
     missing in 100% of records.
     Reasoning: Retaining empty columns increases memory overhead and introduces
     noise. The column was dropped permanently.
   - Other columns with partial missing values (like ExitDate) were retained to
     avoid losing records for active employees.

C. Deduplication:
   - Duplicate records skew statistical measures (mean, variance) and lead to
     inaccurate counts.
   - We audited and removed duplicate records. The duplicate count was reduced
     from the initial level to exactly zero.
   - The cleaned dataset was saved as "cleaned_data_HR.csv".

3. HR DATASET DICTIONARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Key attributes analyzed in this project:
- FirstName / LastName : Employee identification.
- StartDate / ExitDate : Date of hire and date of termination (if applicable).
- Title                : Job title (e.g., Production Technician, Sales Manager).
- Supervisor           : Name of the employee's direct supervisor.
- ADEmail              : Active Directory corporate email address.
- BusinessUnit         : Division of the company (e.g., CCDR, EW, PL, TNS).
- EmployeeStatus       : Current status (Active, Voluntarily Terminated, etc.).
- DepartmentType       : Specific department (Production, Sales, IT, Admin).
- Current Employee Rating: Performance rating score (typically 1 to 5).
- Engagement Score     : Employee engagement survey score (typically 1 to 5).
- Satisfaction Score   : Job satisfaction survey score (typically 1 to 5).
- Work-Life Balance Score: Work-life balance survey score (typically 1 to 5).
- Training Program Name: Name of training program completed.
- Training Cost        : Direct cost of the training course ($).
- Training Duration    : Number of days training lasted.

4. KEY STATISTICAL FINDINGS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
A. Baseline Metrics (Entire Dataset):
   - Average Employee Performance Rating: 2.97 out of 5.0
   - Maximum Employee Performance Rating: 5.0
   - Average Employee Engagement Score:   2.94 out of 5.0

B. Department Performance Rankings:
   The departments ranked by their mean employee performance ratings:
   1. Billing            : 3.50 (Highest average performance)
   2. Data Analyst       : 3.29
   3. Executive Office   : 3.12
   4. Production         : 3.01
   5. Software Engineering: 3.00
   6. HR                 : 2.92
   7. IT Support         : 2.91
   8. Finance            : 2.76
   9. Sales              : 2.72 (Lowest average performance)

C. Job Satisfaction by Business Unit:
   Average satisfaction scores across major business units show consistency,
   ranging between 2.90 and 3.05 out of 5.0.

D. Correlation Analysis:
   - Engagement Score vs. Satisfaction Score:
     Correlation (r) = 0.0167 (Very weak positive relationship)
     Takeaway: Employees who score high on engagement surveys do not necessarily
     report high job satisfaction, and vice versa. HR should investigate both
     metrics independently.
   - Training Duration (Days) vs. Current Employee Rating:
     Correlation (r) = -0.0219 (Very weak negative relationship)
     Takeaway: The duration of training has no positive influence on employee
     performance ratings. Training quality or curriculum alignment may need
     restructuring.

E. Peak Training Cost Event:
   - The highest recorded training cost for a single employee training program
     was identified. HR should audit this program to verify if it yielded a
     commensurate return on investment (ROI).
